Music Projects

The image above was created in Max 7, a visual programming language that speciailizes in computer music. Over the course of the past four months, I have learned the fundamentals of Max and how sounds can be represented moduarly. During this process I created a number of projects, including the fullscreen piano project pictured above and the FM Synthesis project pictured below. The fullscreen piano project involved dividing the screen into a 5 x 8 grid that represents 5 octaves on a keyboard, and attibuting each of those to a certain note than can be activated by hovering over it with the mouse. This allows users to play scales simply by moving their mouse across the screen.



The patch shown above modularly represents an FM synthesis engine, and allows the user to tweak the specifications of the engine by simply changing values representing harmonicity, deformation, and transposition. This allows for a high degree of freedom within the patch, leading to more diverse and unique sounds.
