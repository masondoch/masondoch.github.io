About Me

Mason is a second-year Regents’ and Chancellor’s Scholar at UC Berkeley, intending to major in Computer Science.

Mason is a member of Political Computer Science at Berkeley, which integrates software engineering into the field of political science through detailed projects that involve complex data analysis and application development. His current project explores the relevance of blockchain technology in international government, with the goal of producing a research paper on the topic and an Ethereum-based voting application designed to decrease voter fraud and increase security. He was also a four-year member of the Dos Pueblos Engineering Academy, during which time he developed the software for an interactive, physics-based arcade game that won multiple Editors’ Choice awards at the 2017 Maker Faire.

Mason also has a background in music, and hopes to combine his ever-expanding knowledge of modern music production with his passion for computer science to develop new and innovative technologies to aid in sound design, synthesis, and other popular production techniques.
